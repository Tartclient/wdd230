History

Seattle was originally inhabited by Indigenous people for thousands of years before the arrival of European settlers. In 1851, the first European settlers arrived in Seattle and established a townsite. The town grew rapidly due to its location as a port city, and by the 1880s, it was one of the largest cities on the West Coast. Seattle's economy was originally based on the timber and fishing industries, but it later grew to include shipbuilding, manufacturing, and technology. Companies like Boeing, Amazon, and Microsoft are all based in the Seattle area. Seattle played an important role in the Civil Rights Movement, with local activists fighting for desegregation and equal rights for people of color. The city also played a key role in the labor movement, with workers organizing for better working conditions and higher wages.

Demographic

Population:
According to the U.S. Census Bureau, as of 2020, Seattle has a population of approximately 769,714 people.

Diversity:
Seattle is a diverse city with a population that is approximately 65% White, 15% Asian, 7% Black or African American, and 6% Hispanic or Latino.

Education:
Seattle is known for its highly educated population, with approximately 57% of residents holding a Bachelor's degree or higher.

Income:
The median household income in Seattle is approximately $105,000, which is higher than the national median household income of approximately $68,000.

Space Needle

The iconic Space Needle, a 605-foot tall observation tower, offers stunning 360-degree views of Seattle and its surroundings.

Pike Place Market

One of the oldest public farmers' markets in the United States, Pike Place Market is a bustling hub of fresh produce, artisan crafts, and unique shops.

Chihuly Garden and Glass

This mesmerizing art exhibit showcases the vibrant glass sculptures of renowned artist Dale Chihuly, set within a lush garden environment.

Gas Works Park

A former gas plant turned public park, Gas Works Park offers a unique blend of industrial structures, green spaces, and panoramic views of the city skyline.

Alki Beach

Alki Beach is a popular destination for sunbathing, beach volleyball, and stunning views of the Seattle skyline across Elliott Bay.
